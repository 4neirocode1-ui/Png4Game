ART DIRECTION AND ITEM GENERATION SPECIFICATION
Project Overview

2D dark fantasy tactical roguelike focused on squad-based expeditions, survival, equipment progression, crafting, and tactical turn-based combat.

The visual style must prioritize:

readability over spectacle;
gameplay clarity over realism;
strong silhouettes;
clear item recognition;
consistent visual language across all assets.

The world is harsh, grounded, and dangerous. Equipment is practical, functional, and believable.

CORE VISUAL PILLARS
1. Readability First

Every generated asset must remain recognizable at gameplay scale.

Requirements:

strong silhouette recognition;
clear overall shape;
immediate visual identification;
minimal visual noise;
easy distinction between categories.

Effects, decoration, and artistic flourishes must never reduce readability.

2. Grounded Dark Fantasy

The setting is low fantasy rather than high fantasy.

Avoid:

anime aesthetics;
oversized weapons;
exaggerated proportions;
excessive magical glow;
ornate fantasy decorations;
unrealistic heroic designs.

Prefer:

practical medieval equipment;
believable materials;
iron, steel, leather, wood, cloth;
realistic construction;
functional craftsmanship.
3. Gameplay-Oriented Asset Design

Assets are gameplay elements first and illustrations second.

Every icon should communicate:

what the item is;
what category it belongs to;
how it differs from similar items.

A player should identify an item type instantly without reading text.

ITEM ICON PRODUCTION RULES
Primary Goal

Item icons are gameplay assets.

Visual beauty is secondary to recognition speed.

A player must correctly identify an item at a glance when displayed at 64×64 pixels.

Canvas Requirements

Background:

pure white (#FFFFFF).

Forbidden:

grids;
inventory slots;
decorative frames;
borders;
labels;
text;
numbers;
symbols;
watermarks;
UI elements;
interface mockups.

Only the item itself should be visible.

Readability Requirements

Icons must remain readable at 64×64 pixels.

Requirements:

strong silhouette;
clear outline;
recognizable shape;
large readable forms;
minimal clutter.

Avoid:

excessive decoration;
tiny details;
visual noise;
microscopic engravings;
unnecessary complexity.

The item must remain recognizable from silhouette alone.

Shape Language

Different categories must be distinguishable primarily through shape.

Recognition must never depend solely on color.

Examples:

Healing Potion:

rounded bottle;
stable silhouette;
positive visual language.

Poison:

angular bottle;
dangerous silhouette;
aggressive shape language.

Mana Potion:

clearly different bottle shape from healing and poison.

Buff Potions:

unique silhouettes distinct from both healing and poison.

The difference between consumables should be obvious at first glance.

Weapon Recognition

Weapon classes must be identifiable through silhouette.

Differences must not rely only on:

scale;
color;
rotation;
camera angle.

Each weapon category requires distinct geometry.

Examples:

Dagger:

short;
narrow blade.

Sword:

balanced proportions.

Greatsword:

long two-handed silhouette.

Axe:

dominant cutting head.

Hammer:

dominant striking head.

Spear:

long shaft;
compact spearhead.

Mace:

compact impact-focused head.

A player should identify weapon type instantly without reading its name.

Item Condition

All items are assumed to be intact and functional.

Do not depict:

cracks;
broken blades;
missing pieces;
damaged structures;
severe corrosion;
heavy wear.

Exceptions are allowed only when damage is explicitly mentioned in the item's name or description.

Examples:

Allowed:

Broken Sword;
Cracked Shield;
Rusted Axe;
Damaged Helmet.

Not Allowed:

random damage on normal equipment;
unnecessary cracks or wear on intact items.
Visual Focus

The item should occupy most of the image.

Recommended:

70–90% of canvas area.

Avoid:

excessive empty space;
distant framing;
tiny objects.

The object must immediately draw attention.

Lighting

Use neutral studio-style lighting.

Avoid:

dramatic shadows;
environmental lighting;
colored lighting;
atmospheric fog;
scene-based illumination.

The item itself must remain clearly visible.

Perspective

Use a consistent perspective across all icons.

Requirements:

identical camera logic;
consistent viewing angle;
consistent visual scale.

All inventory assets should appear as part of the same asset library.

Materials

Materials should be realistic and easily recognizable.

Examples:

Metal:

steel;
iron;
bronze.

Organic:

leather;
wood;
bone;
cloth.

Materials must remain visually readable even at small icon sizes.

Color Usage

Color supports recognition but must never be the only identifier.

Silhouette and shape remain the primary recognition tools.

Examples:

Healing:

red accents acceptable.

Poison:

green accents acceptable.

Mana:

blue accents acceptable.

However, categories must still remain distinguishable in grayscale.

Consistency Requirements

All generated assets must appear to belong to the same game.

Requirements:

consistent rendering style;
consistent lighting;
consistent perspective;
consistent material treatment;
consistent detail density;
consistent visual quality.

The complete inventory should feel like a unified professional asset set.

ABSOLUTE PRIORITY ORDER

When a conflict occurs, follow this priority:

Readability.
Recognition speed.
Gameplay clarity.
Consistency.
Visual beauty.
Artistic detail.

Gameplay readability always takes precedence over artistic complexity.

SHEET OUTPUT LAYOUT (BATCH GENERATION — for automated slicing)

Items are generated several-per-image as a sheet, then a script cuts each item
into a separate transparent icon. To make automated slicing reliable, the
output image MUST follow these rules.

Background:

pure flat white (#FFFFFF) across the WHOLE image;
no gradients;
no texture;
no vignette;
no off-white or grey tint.

Layout:

arrange items on a regular grid (even rows and columns);
one item per cell, centered in its cell;
consistent cell size and consistent row height;
wide white gutters between items AND between rows;
items must never touch, overlap, or share an outline;
equal empty white padding around every item.

Strictly forbidden on the sheet:

labels, captions, names, text, numbers, watermarks of any kind;
drop shadows, cast shadows, ground, pedestals, or reflections under items;
frames, grid lines, slot borders, separators between cells.

Outline and contrast (critical for the cutter):

every item must be clearly darker than the white background;
every item must have a continuous, closed darker outline around its whole silhouette;
light or metallic parts (steel blades, highlights) must stay INSIDE that outline, never bleeding into the white background;
avoid near-white item areas that touch the background without a dark edge.

Consistency:

identical camera angle, lighting, and scale logic for all items on the sheet;
the whole sheet must read as one unified asset library.

Reasoning: the slicer separates each item by its silhouette, drops the white
background to full transparency, and exports clean per-item icons. Anything
that breaks the white gutters (shadows, labels, touching items) will corrupt
the cut.
