PART 1 — STYLE (copy this whole part into every request)
==================================================================

1. WHAT THIS IS
- These are inventory item icons for a 2D dark-fantasy game.
- You draw several items together on one sheet (a grid). Each item sits alone in its own cell.

2. RENDERING STYLE — Darkest Dungeon
- 2D hand-painted illustration in the Darkest Dungeon style.
- One heavy, dark, continuous ink outline around the whole item.
- Flat, even lighting. The same light direction for every item.
- Painterly, gritty texture. A muted, grim, desaturated palette.
- Slightly exaggerated, stylized proportions.
- This is a painted 2D icon. It is not a photo. It is not a 3D render.

3. READABILITY — the icon is shown at 64x64 pixels
- The item is recognized instantly from its silhouette alone at 64x64.
- Exaggerate the one shape that defines the item. Make that feature big and obvious.
- Use large, bold forms. No tiny details, no fine engraving, no clutter.
- A player names the item at a glance, with zero hesitation.

4. EVERY ITEM IS A DIFFERENT THING
- Every item reads as a clearly different object from every other item.
- Two items are never the same thing at two sizes. Change the shape, not just the scale.
- Leave no room to wonder "sword or dagger? potion or oil? poison or regen?".

5. COLOR LAW — use these exact meanings everywhere
- Health and healing = red.
- Mana = blue.
- Poison = green.
- Fire = orange.
- Frost and cold = pale cyan.
- Shock and lightning = yellow.
- Water and wet = teal.
- Stun = white.
- Color supports recognition. Shape carries it: the silhouette alone already tells the category.

6. CONDITION
- Every item is intact, clean and functional. No cracks, no broken parts, no rust, no heavy wear.

7. CANVAS AND BACKGROUND
- Pure flat white background (#FFFFFF) across the whole image. No gradient, no texture, no tint.
- The item fills about 70 to 90 percent of its cell, centered.
- A real see-through hole in an item (the inside of a ring) is filled with the same pure white.

8. SHEET LAYOUT — the image is a grid of items; a script cuts each one out
- Arrange the items on a regular grid: equal cell size, equal column width, equal row height.
- One item per cell, centered in its cell.
- Wide white gutters between items and between rows. A gutter is at least 25 percent of a cell.
- Items never touch, never overlap, never share an outline, not even at the corners.
- The same number of items in every row. Fill the grid in order. Empty cells go only at the bottom-right.
- Reading order is left-to-right, then top-to-bottom. The script numbers icons in this order.

9. LONG ITEMS GO DIAGONAL
- Draw every long, thin item at a 45-degree diagonal: blades, shafts, bows, arrows, bolts.
- No item is more than 3 times longer in one direction than the other. The diagonal keeps it square.

10. OUTLINE AND CONTRAST — the cutter needs this
- Every item is clearly darker than the white background.
- Every item has one continuous, closed, dark outline around its whole silhouette.
- All bright or metallic parts (steel blades, highlights) stay inside that dark outline.
- No part of the item fades to near-white where it meets the background.

11. FORBIDDEN — never draw any of these
- Text, letters, numbers, names, labels, captions, watermarks.
- Drop shadows, cast shadows, ground, pedestals, reflections under the item.
- Frames, borders, grid lines, slot outlines, separators.
- Background scenery, props, hands, characters.

PART 3 — STATUS / EFFECT ICONS (different from items — read this)
==================================================================

RU: статусы висят маленькими значками на портретах. Генерим крупно (как предметы),
потом обработчик сожмёт до 24-48px. Поэтому символ должен ПЕРЕЖИВАТЬ сильное сжатие.
slug в скобках мапится на icon_registry.gd (значки статусов), не на предметы.

STATUS RULES (these replace the "item shape" rules; PART 1 style, color law,
white background and sheet-layout rules still apply):
- A status icon is ONE bold SYMBOL or emblem, not an object scene.
- It will be shrunk to 24-48px. It must NOT turn to mush. Use thick chunky shapes,
  one strong silhouette, heavy outline, high contrast. No thin lines, no small parts,
  no fine detail. The symbol fills the cell.
- Color follows the COLOR LAW from PART 1. The symbol shape alone already tells which status.
- HEAVY rule: a HEAVY status is the SAME base symbol, amped up — drawn bigger and bolder,
  with one bright RED accent mark added. Same symbol, stronger reading.
- COMBO rule: a combo-poison is the GREEN poison color blended with one element color,
  on a symbol that merges the two.
